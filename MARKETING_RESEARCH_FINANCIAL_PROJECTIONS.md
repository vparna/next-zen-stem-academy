**Break-Even Students = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin**  
**Break-Even Students = $7,005 / $63 = 112 students**

## 8. Risk Analysis

### 8.1 Key Risks and Mitigation Strategies

**1. Slower Than Projected Enrollment**
- **Risk Level:** High
- **Impact:** Extended break-even timeline
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Aggressive marketing in first 90 days
  - Early-bird discounts
  - Referral incentive program ($50 credit)
  - Free trial classes

**2. Higher Operating Costs**
- **Risk Level:** Medium
- **Impact:** Reduced profitability
- **Mitigation:**
  - Negotiate longer lease terms for better rates
  - Energy-efficient equipment
  - Bulk purchasing for materials
  - Part-time vs. full-time instructor mix

**3. Competitive Pressure**
- **Risk Level:** Medium
- **Impact:** Price pressure, student loss
- **Mitigation:**
  - Differentiation through 3S philosophy
  - Superior curriculum and platform
  - Build strong community
  - Focus on outcomes and certifications

**4. Seasonal Fluctuations**
- **Risk Level:** Low
- **Impact:** Revenue variability
- **Mitigation:**
  - Summer camp programs
  - Holiday workshops
  - Flexible course start dates
  - Online course options

**5. Instructor Turnover**
- **Risk Level:** Medium
- **Impact:** Quality and consistency
- **Mitigation:**
  - Competitive compensation
  - Professional development
  - Clear advancement path
  - Backup instructor pool

### Appendix D: Glossary

**Break-Even Point:** The point at which total revenue equals total expenses  
**Contribution Margin:** Revenue per student minus variable costs per student  
**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV):** Average total revenue per student over their enrollment period  
**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Marketing and sales costs divided by new enrollments  
**Gross Margin:** (Revenue - Variable Costs) / Revenue  
**Net Margin:** Net Income / Revenue  
**Retention Rate:** Percentage of students who continue from one course to the next
